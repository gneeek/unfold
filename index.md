This site is for practices that reveal their shape by being built. Each note is an attempt to describe something only after doing it, not before.

## What this is

unfold is a working collection of notes, dialogues, and patterns from a practice of building software with coding agents. Some of what we know about this work is solid enough to generalize. A lot of it is still rough. This site is where both kinds live together, and where the rough work becomes less rough through use on real projects.

We write mostly for ourselves on future projects, for other practitioners trying similar work, and as a side effect for coding agents that need a clear map of what we believe and why.

## Philosophy and principles

Two principles carry most of the weight right now. More will be added as they earn their place here through use.

**Small batches.** Ship frequently in small, focused steps. One to three goals per release, short-lived branches, work sized to be reviewed and reverted without drama.

**Fast feedback loops.** Observe, orient, decide, act. The cycle between deciding what to build and seeing how it lands should be as short as the task allows. Shorter cycles reveal mistakes earlier and make them cheaper to fix.
