Reflective writing about the practice itself. Retrospectives, dialogues, explainers of how the tools actually work, and other working notes that do not yet fit on a principles page.

- **[Two perspectives on harness engineering in TDF26](/unfold/notes/2026-04-10-harness-engineering-dialogue/)** (2026-04-10). The originating dialogue, in two passes plus a publisher rebuttal, on how harness-engineering patterns from HumanLayer and OpenAI apply to a real small-batch project.
- **[Agent ownership and the naming of Piers and Tully](/unfold/notes/2026-04-10-agent-ownership-and-names/)** (2026-04-10). How the project adopted a two-agent ownership model for its repositories, and where the names Piers and Tully came from.
- **[Personas versus subagents: how Piers and Tully actually run in Claude Code](/unfold/notes/2026-04-10-personas-vs-subagents/)** (2026-04-10). A short explainer clarifying the difference between named personas and real Claude Code subagents, arising from a real question during planning.
- **[Loops inside loops: OODA at multiple scales](/unfold/notes/2026-04-11-nested-ooda-loops/)** (2026-04-11). How retrospective, planning, and sprint map onto Boyd's OODA loop at the macro scale, and how each step contains its own smaller OODA loop inside it.
- **[Parallel branches, parallel sessions](/unfold/notes/2026-04-11-parallel-branches-parallel-sessions/)** (2026-04-11). Two patterns for keeping parallel streams of work moving without blocking: git worktrees for branch isolation, and issue comments for lightweight coordination between sessions.
- **[Ceremonies on the OODA loop: retro, planning, sprint](/unfold/notes/2026-04-20-ceremonies-on-the-ooda-loop/)** (2026-04-20). A sharper reading of the retro/planning/sprint mapping — orient bridges retro and planning, which is why retro-before-planning is structural rather than conventional, and which tells you when to run a retro.
- **[The last responsible moment under a permanence rule](/unfold/notes/2026-04-20-last-responsible-moment-and-permanence/)** (2026-04-20). Poppendieck's last responsible moment is a judgement call unless the project has a permanence rule — a content-change rule, append-only log, or immutable tag — that defines the moment sharply and gives the review window structural weight.
